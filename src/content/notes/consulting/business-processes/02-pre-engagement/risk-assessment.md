# Risk Assessment Guide

## Overview

This document provides guidance on conducting risk assessments during the pre-engagement phase. A thorough risk assessment helps identify potential issues before committing to an engagement.

---

## Risk Assessment Categories

### 1. Client Risk

| Factor | Assessment Questions | Risk Level |
|--------|---------------------|------------|
| **Financial Stability** | Is the client financially stable? Any recent issues? | H/M/L |
| **Reputation** | Any negative press or regulatory issues? | H/M/L |
| **Industry** | Is the industry high-risk (e.g., gambling, weapons)? | H/M/L |
| **Geography** | Any high-risk jurisdictions involved? | H/M/L |
| **Relationship History** | Any prior issues with the firm engagements? | H/M/L |

### 2. Engagement Risk

| Factor | Assessment Questions | Risk Level |
|--------|---------------------|------------|
| **Scope Clarity** | Are requirements well-defined? | H/M/L |
| **Timeline** | Is the timeline realistic? | H/M/L |
| **Budget** | Is the budget adequate for scope? | H/M/L |
| **Complexity** | Technical or organizational complexity? | H/M/L |
| **Dependencies** | Heavy reliance on client or third parties? | H/M/L |

### 3. Delivery Risk

| Factor | Assessment Questions | Risk Level |
|--------|---------------------|------------|
| **Team Capability** | Do we have the right skills? | H/M/L |
| **Resource Availability** | Are resources available when needed? | H/M/L |
| **Technology** | Proven technology or experimental? | H/M/L |
| **Data Quality** | Is client data likely to be usable? | H/M/L |
| **Integration** | Complex integrations required? | H/M/L |

### 4. Commercial Risk

| Factor | Assessment Questions | Risk Level |
|--------|---------------------|------------|
| **Pricing Model** | Fixed fee with unclear scope? | H/M/L |
| **Payment Terms** | Unusual or risky payment terms? | H/M/L |
| **Liability** | Unlimited liability exposure? | H/M/L |
| **IP Issues** | Complex IP ownership requirements? | H/M/L |

## Risk Scoring

### Risk Level Definitions

| Level | Definition | Score |
|-------|------------|-------|
| **High (H)** | Significant concern, requires mitigation | 3 |
| **Medium (M)** | Moderate concern, monitor closely | 2 |
| **Low (L)** | Minor concern, standard management | 1 |

### Overall Risk Classification

| Total Score | Classification | Action Required |
|-------------|----------------|-----------------|
| 0-15 | Low Risk | Standard approval process |
| 16-25 | Medium Risk | Manager review required |
| 26-35 | High Risk | Partner approval required |
| 36+ | Very High Risk | Senior partner/risk review |

---

## Risk Assessment Process

### Step 1: Information Gathering
- Review client background
- Analyze engagement requirements
- Assess team and resource situation
- Review similar past engagements

### Step 2: Risk Identification
- Complete risk assessment checklist
- Consult with subject matter experts
- Review with pursuit team

### Step 3: Risk Evaluation
- Score each risk factor
- Calculate overall risk level
- Identify top risks

### Step 4: Mitigation Planning
- Develop mitigation strategies for high risks
- Document assumptions and conditions
- Define monitoring approach

### Step 5: Approval and Documentation
- Obtain required approvals
- Document assessment results
- File in engagement records

---

## Risk Mitigation Strategies

| Risk Type | Common Mitigations |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **Scope Risk** | Detailed SOW, change control process |
| **Timeline Risk** | Buffer time, phased approach |
| **Resource Risk** | Backup resources, early staffing |
| **Technical Risk** | Proof of concept, technical spikes |
| **Commercial Risk** | Capped fees, milestone payments |

---

## Required Approvals

| Risk Level | Approval Authority |
|------------|-------------------|
| Low | Engagement Manager |
| Medium | Engagement Partner |
| High | Service Line Leader |
| Very High | Market Risk Leader |
