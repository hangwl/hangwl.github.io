# Independence and Conflicts

## Overview

Independence and conflict management are fundamental to the firm's professional standards. This document outlines the requirements and processes for ensuring compliance before accepting any engagement.

---

## Why Independence Matters

- **Professional Standards:** Required by audit and professional regulations
- **Client Trust:** Maintains objectivity and credibility
- **Legal Protection:** Protects firm and clients from liability
- **Reputation:** Preserves Firm brand integrity

---

## Independence Requirements

### Types of Independence

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Independence of Mind** | State of mind that permits objective professional judgment |
| **Independence in Appearance** | Avoidance of circumstances that would cause a reasonable third party to question objectivity |

### When Independence Applies

Independence requirements are **strictest** for:
- Audit clients
- Attestation engagements
- Certain regulatory filings

For **advisory/consulting engagements**:
- Independence rules still apply but may be less restrictive
- Must not impair audit independence if client is also an audit client
- Must check for business relationships and conflicts

---

## Global Independence Monitoring System (GIMS)

### Process Overview

1. **Initiate Check:** Submit independence request in GIMS
2. **Entity Identification:** Identify all relevant entities (client and related parties)
3. **Relationship Review:** System checks Firm relationships
4. **Analysis:** Independence team reviews potential issues
5. **Clearance:** Approval or conditions issued

### Information Required

- Client legal entity name
- Ultimate parent company
- Related entities in scope
- Nature of proposed services
- Engagement team members
- Anticipated start date

### Timeline

- Standard requests: 2-5 business days
- Complex requests: Up to 10 business days
- Expedited requests: Available for urgent situations

---

## Conflict Checks

### Types of Conflicts

1. **Direct Conflicts:** Work for competing clients on conflicting matters
2. **Indirect Conflicts:** Perceived conflicts or appearance issues
3. **Information Barriers:** Access to confidential information from other engagements

### Conflict Check Process

1. Submit conflict check request
2. Identify nature of work and parties involved
3. System searches for potential conflicts
4. Review and resolution
5. Clearance or mitigation requirements

### Conflict Mitigation Options

If conflicts are identified:
- **Ethical Walls:** Information barriers between teams
- **Informed Consent:** Client acknowledgment of potential conflict
- **Team Restrictions:** Exclude conflicted individuals
- **Engagement Decline:** If conflict cannot be mitigated

---

## Common Independence Issues

### Business Relationships
- Firm holding investments in client
- Joint business ventures
- Vendor/supplier relationships
- Board or governance positions

### Personal Relationships
- Team members with family at client
- Close personal relationships
- Prior employment at client

### Financial Relationships
- Direct investments in client securities
- Loans to/from client
- Business relationships with client executives

### Employment Relationships
- Former Firm personnel at client
- Potential employment discussions
- Secondment arrangements

---

## Red Flags

Be alert to these warning signs:
- Client is an audit client of EY
- Work involves valuation that could affect financial statements
- Management decision-making responsibilities
- Acting in legal capacity for client
- Significant financial interest in outcome
- Contingent fee arrangements (for certain clients)

---

## Required Actions

### Before Every Engagement

- [ ] Submit independence check in GIMS
- [ ] Complete conflict check
- [ ] Wait for clearance before signing contract
- [ ] Document any conditions or restrictions

### During Engagement

- [ ] Monitor for changes in relationships
- [ ] Report new potential conflicts
- [ ] Maintain required information barriers
- [ ] Ensure team compliance

### If Issues Arise

1. **Stop** activities that may compromise independence
2. **Report** to independence team immediately
3. **Consult** with engagement partner and legal
4. **Document** the issue and resolution
