# Proposal Development

## Overview

This guide covers the process of developing winning proposals for Data and AI engagements, from initial scoping through submission.

---

## Proposal Types

### 1. Proactive Proposals
- Unsolicited proposals based on identified client needs
- More flexibility in structure and approach
- Opportunity to shape client thinking

### 2. Reactive Proposals (RFP Response)
- Response to formal client RFP/RFQ
- Must follow client-specified format
- Strict deadlines and evaluation criteria

### 3. Follow-on Proposals
- Extensions or new phases of existing work
- Leverage existing relationships and knowledge
- Often streamlined approval process

---

## Proposal Development Process

### Step 1: Kickoff and Planning (Day 1-2)

**Activities:**
- Assemble proposal team
- Review client requirements / RFP
- Clarify questions with client (if allowed)
- Develop proposal outline
- Assign section owners
- Create timeline with milestones

**Outputs:**
- Proposal plan
- Section assignments
- Key messages and themes

---

### Step 2: Discovery and Scoping (Day 2-5)

**Activities:**
- Client workshops or calls (if possible)
- Requirements analysis
- Current state assessment
- Stakeholder mapping
- Constraint and dependency identification

**Outputs:**
- Requirements document
- Scope definition
- Assumptions and exclusions

---

### Step 3: Solution Design (Day 3-7)

**Activities:**
- Develop technical approach
- Design solution architecture
- Define methodology and phases
- Identify tools and technologies
- Create high-level project plan
- Define deliverables

**Outputs:**
- Solution architecture
- Project approach
- Deliverables list
- High-level timeline

---

### Step 4: Team and Pricing (Day 5-8)

**Activities:**
- Design team structure
- Identify named resources
- Estimate effort by role/phase
- Develop pricing model
- Calculate margins
- Obtain pricing approval

**Outputs:**
- Team structure and bios
- Effort estimates
- Pricing breakdown
- Commercial terms

---

### Step 5: Writing and Assembly (Day 6-10)

**Activities:**
- Draft all sections
- Integrate team inputs
- Add case studies and credentials
- Create visuals and diagrams
- Compile appendices
- Format and brand compliance

**Key Sections:**
1. Executive Summary
2. Understanding of Requirements
3. Proposed Approach and Methodology
4. Solution Design
5. Project Plan and Timeline
6. Team and Qualifications
7. Relevant Experience
8. Commercial Proposal
9. Terms and Conditions
10. Appendices

---

### Step 6: Review and Refinement (Day 8-11)

**Review Levels:**

| Review Type | Reviewer | Focus |
|-------------|----------|-------|
| **Technical Review** | Solution Architect | Technical accuracy and feasibility |
| **Commercial Review** | Pricing Lead | Pricing accuracy and competitiveness |
| **Quality Review** | Independent Reviewer | Overall quality and compliance |
| **Partner Review** | Engagement Partner | Strategic alignment and client fit |

**Common Review Criteria:**
- Responsiveness to requirements
- Clarity and readability
- Differentiation from competitors
- Realistic commitments
- Risk identification and mitigation
- Commercial viability

---

### Step 7: Finalization and Submission (Day 11-12)

**Activities:**
- Incorporate review feedback
- Final proofreading
- Compliance check (for RFPs)
- Partner sign-off
- Submission preparation
- Timely delivery

---

## Proposal Writing Best Practices

### Do's
- **Lead with value:** Focus on client outcomes and benefits
- **Be specific:** Use concrete examples and metrics
- **Show understanding:** Demonstrate knowledge of client's business
- **Differentiate:** Highlight what makes Firm unique
- **Use visuals:** Diagrams, charts, and infographics enhance readability
- **Be concise:** Respect the reader's time
- **Proofread:** Errors undermine credibility

### Don'ts
- Don't use excessive jargon
- Don't make generic claims without evidence
- Don't overpromise or underestimate effort
- Don't ignore RFP instructions
- Don't submit late
- Don't copy-paste from old proposals without customization
