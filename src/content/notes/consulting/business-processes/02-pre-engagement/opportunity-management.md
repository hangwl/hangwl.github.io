# Opportunity Management

## Overview

This document covers the process of identifying, qualifying, and managing business opportunities in the Data and AI practice.

---

## Opportunity Sources

### 1. Existing Client Relationships
- Follow-on work from current engagements
- Cross-selling from other Firm service lines
- Account mining and expansion
- Client referrals

### 2. New Client Acquisition
- Partner network and referrals
- Industry events and conferences
- Thought leadership and publications
- Digital marketing and inbound leads

### 3. Formal Procurement
- Request for Proposal (RFP)
- Request for Information (RFI)
- Request for Quotation (RFQ)
- Government tenders

## Opportunity Qualification

### BANT Framework

Before pursuing an opportunity, assess:

| Criteria | Key Questions |
|----------|---------------|
| **Budget** | Does the client have budget allocated? What is the expected investment range? |
| **Authority** | Who is the decision-maker? Do we have access to them? |
| **Need** | Is there a genuine business need? What is driving this initiative? |
| **Timeline** | When does the client want to start? Is there a deadline? |

### Go/No-Go Checklist

- [ ] Opportunity aligns with the firm capabilities and strategy
- [ ] Client passes independence and conflict checks
- [ ] Reasonable win probability (>30%)
- [ ] Acceptable risk profile
- [ ] Resource availability for pursuit and delivery
- [ ] Commercially viable (meets margin thresholds)
- [ ] No reputational concerns

## CRM and Pipeline Management

### CRM (Firm CRM System)

All opportunities must be registered in CRM:

**Required Information:**
- Client name and contacts
- Opportunity description
- Estimated deal value
- Expected close date
- Sales stage
- Probability percentage
- Assigned pursuit team

### Sales Stages

| Stage | Description | Probability |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| **1. Identified** | Opportunity identified, not yet qualified | 10% |
| **2. Qualified** | Meets qualification criteria, pursuing | 25% |
| **3. Proposal Submitted** | Formal proposal delivered to client | 50% |
| **4. Shortlisted** | Confirmed on client shortlist | 70% |
| **5. Verbal Win** | Client verbal commitment received | 90% |
| **6. Contracted** | Contract signed | 100% |
| **Lost** | Opportunity lost or withdrawn | 0% |

## Pursuit Team Formation

For significant opportunities, form a pursuit team:

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| **Pursuit Lead** | Overall pursuit coordination |
| **Client Partner** | Client relationship and access |
| **Solution Lead** | Technical solution development |
| **Proposal Manager** | Proposal document coordination |
| **Subject Matter Experts** | Domain-specific input |
| **Pricing Lead** | Commercial model development |

---

## Key Activities by Stage

### Stage 1-2: Identification and Qualification
- Initial client meetings
- Needs assessment
- Capability presentations
- Independence check initiation

### Stage 3: Proposal Development
- Detailed requirements gathering
- Solution design workshops
- Proposal writing and reviews
- Pricing and contract preparation

### Stage 4: Shortlist and Negotiation
- Client presentations and orals
- Reference calls
- Commercial negotiations
- Contract finalization

### Stage 5-6: Contracting
- Legal review and approval
- Contract execution
- Transition to delivery team

---

## Win/Loss Analysis

After every significant pursuit:

**For Wins:**
- Document key success factors
- Capture competitive insights
- Record client decision criteria
- Archive reusable proposal content

**For Losses:**
- Request client feedback
- Analyze competitive positioning
- Identify improvement areas
- Update pursuit playbook

---

## Metrics and Reporting

Track and report on:
- Pipeline value by stage
- Win rate by segment/solution
- Average deal size
- Sales cycle length
- Pursuit cost vs. deal value
