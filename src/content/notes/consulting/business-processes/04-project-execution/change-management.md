# Change Management

## Overview

Change management ensures that modifications to project scope, schedule, or budget are properly evaluated, approved, and implemented. This document covers both project change control and organizational change management.

---

## Part 1: Project Change Control

### Types of Changes

| Change Type | Description | Examples |
|-------------|-------------|----------|
| **Scope Change** | Modification to deliverables or requirements | New features, removed functionality |
| **Schedule Change** | Adjustment to timeline or milestones | Deadline extension, phase reordering |
| **Budget Change** | Modification to project cost | Additional resources, reduced scope |
| **Resource Change** | Changes to team composition | Team member replacement, role changes |
| **Technical Change** | Technology or approach modifications | Platform change, architecture revision |

---

### Change Control Process

```
1. Change Identification
   ↓
2. Change Request Submission
   ↓
3. Impact Assessment
   ↓
4. Review and Approval
   ↓
5. Implementation
   ↓
6. Documentation and Communication
```

---

### Step 1: Change Identification

**Sources of Change:**
- Client requests
- Technical discoveries
- Risk materialization
- Regulatory changes
- Team recommendations

**Initial Assessment:**
- Is this a genuine change or clarification?
- What triggered this request?
- What is the urgency?

---

### Step 2: Change Request Submission

**Required Information:**
- Change description
- Business justification
- Requested by
- Date requested
- Priority/urgency
- Initial impact estimate

---

### Step 3: Impact Assessment

Evaluate impact across dimensions:

| Dimension | Assessment Questions |
|-----------|---------------------|
| **Scope** | What deliverables are affected? |
| **Schedule** | How does this affect timeline? |
| **Budget** | What is the cost impact? |
| **Resources** | Are additional resources needed? |
| **Quality** | Any quality implications? |
| **Risk** | Does this introduce new risks? |
| **Dependencies** | What else is affected? |

**Effort Estimation:**
- Additional hours required
- Resource types needed
- Duration impact

---

### Step 4: Review and Approval

**Approval Authority Matrix:**

| Change Magnitude | Approver | Turnaround |
|------------------|----------|------------|
| Minor (<8 hours, no cost) | Project Manager | 1 day |
| Moderate (8-40 hours) | Engagement Manager | 3 days |
| Significant (>40 hours) | Engagement Partner + Client Sponsor | 1 week |
| Major (>$50K or >4 weeks) | Steering Committee | 2 weeks |

**Approval Outcomes:**
- **Approved:** Proceed with implementation
- **Approved with modifications:** Adjust and proceed
- **Deferred:** Evaluate for future phase
- **Rejected:** Document and close

---

### Step 5: Implementation

- Update project plan
- Adjust resource allocations
- Modify deliverable specifications
- Update budget and timeline
- Communicate to team

---

### Step 6: Documentation

**Update the following:**
- Change Log
- Project Plan
- Scope Document
- Budget Tracker
- Risk Register (if applicable)

## Part 2: Organizational Change Management

For engagements involving significant business transformation:

### Change Management Framework

**Prosci ADKAR Model:**

| Stage | Focus | Activities |
|-------|-------|------------|
| **A**wareness | Why change is needed | Communications, town halls |
| **D**esire | Motivation to change | Leadership engagement, incentives |
| **K**nowledge | How to change | Training, job aids |
| **A**bility | Capability to change | Practice, coaching, support |
| **R**einforcement | Sustaining change | Recognition, feedback, accountability |

---

### Key Change Management Activities

**1. Stakeholder Analysis**
- Identify all impacted stakeholders
- Assess current state and readiness
- Determine support/resistance levels
- Develop engagement strategies

**2. Communication Planning**
- Key messages by audience
- Communication channels
- Frequency and timing
- Feedback mechanisms

**3. Training and Support**
- Training needs assessment
- Training content development
- Delivery approach (classroom, e-learning, etc.)
- Support materials and job aids

**4. Adoption Monitoring**
- Adoption metrics
- Feedback collection
- Resistance identification
- Course correction

---

### Change Impact Assessment

| Impact Area | Low | Medium | High |
|-------------|-----|--------|------|
| Process changes | Minor tweaks | Moderate changes | Complete redesign |
| Technology | Same tools | New features | New platform |
| Skills required | Existing skills | Some upskilling | Significant training |
| Organizational | Same structure | Role adjustments | Restructuring |
| Culture | Minimal impact | Behavioral change | Mindset shift |
