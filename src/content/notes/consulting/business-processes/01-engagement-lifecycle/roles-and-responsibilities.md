# Roles and Responsibilities

## Overview

This document outlines the key roles involved in Data and AI engagements and their respective responsibilities throughout the project lifecycle.

---

## Team Roles

### Engagement Partner (EP)

**Level:** Partner

**Primary Responsibilities:**
- Overall accountability for engagement success
- Client relationship ownership
- Quality and risk oversight
- Commercial decisions and approvals
- Final sign-off on major deliverables
- Independence and ethics compliance

**Time Commitment:** 5-15% of engagement

**Key Interactions:**
- Client C-suite / Sponsors
- firm leadership
- Quality reviewers

---

### Engagement Manager (EM)

**Level:** Senior Manager / Manager

**Primary Responsibilities:**
- Day-to-day engagement management
- Project planning and scheduling
- Team coordination and management
- Client communication and status reporting
- Budget and financial management
- Issue escalation and resolution
- Quality assurance coordination

**Time Commitment:** 30-50% of engagement

**Key Interactions:**
- Client project managers
- Project team members
- Engagement Partner
- Support functions (Finance, Legal)

---

### Technical Lead / Solution Architect

**Level:** Manager / Senior Consultant

**Primary Responsibilities:**
- Technical solution design
- Architecture decisions
- Technical quality assurance
- Technical risk identification
- Team technical guidance
- Client technical alignment

**Time Commitment:** 50-80% of engagement

**Key Interactions:**
- Client technical stakeholders
- Development team
- Engagement Manager

---

### Senior Consultant

**Level:** Senior Consultant

**Primary Responsibilities:**
- Workstream leadership
- Detailed analysis and design
- Deliverable development
- Junior team mentoring
- Client workshop facilitation
- Technical implementation oversight

**Time Commitment:** 80-100% of engagement

**Key Interactions:**
- Client subject matter experts
- Project team
- Technical Lead

---

### Consultant / Associate

**Level:** Consultant / Associate

**Primary Responsibilities:**
- Task execution and analysis
- Data gathering and processing
- Documentation and reporting
- Research and benchmarking
- Meeting support and note-taking
- Deliverable component development

**Time Commitment:** 100% of engagement

**Key Interactions:**
- Senior Consultant / Manager
- Client working-level staff

---

## Client Roles

### Executive Sponsor

**Responsibilities:**
- Strategic direction and vision
- Budget approval and oversight
- Organizational change championship
- Executive stakeholder management
- Issue escalation resolution

---

### Project Sponsor

**Responsibilities:**
- Day-to-day project oversight
- Decision-making authority
- Resource allocation
- Scope and priority decisions
- Deliverable acceptance

---

### Client Project Manager

**Responsibilities:**
- Internal coordination
- Access and logistics
- Internal communication
- Issue identification
- Schedule alignment

---

### Subject Matter Experts (SMEs)

**Responsibilities:**
- Domain knowledge sharing
- Requirements clarification
- Solution validation
- User acceptance testing
- Change adoption

## RACI Matrix Template

| Activity | EP | EM | Tech Lead | Sr. Consultant | Consultant | Client Sponsor | Client PM |
|----------|----|----|-----------|----------------|------------|----------------|-----------|
| Proposal Development | A | R | C | C | I | I | I |
| Contract Signing | A | R | I | I | I | A | I |
| Project Planning | A | R | C | C | I | C | C |
| Technical Design | C | A | R | C | C | I | I |
| Deliverable Development | I | A | C | R | R | I | I |
| Quality Review | A | R | C | C | I | I | I |
| Status Reporting | I | R | C | C | I | I | C |
| Issue Escalation | A | R | C | I | I | A | C |
| Change Approval | A | R | C | I | I | A | C |
| Project Closure | A | R | C | C | I | A | C |

**Legend:**
- **R** = Responsible (does the work)
- **A** = Accountable (final authority)
- **C** = Consulted (provides input)
- **I** = Informed (kept updated)

---

## Role Transitions

During an engagement, role transitions may occur:

1. **Sales to Delivery:** Ensure proper handover from BD/sales team to delivery team
2. **Team Changes:** Document knowledge transfer when team members rotate
3. **Escalation Paths:** Clear escalation for both firm and client sides
