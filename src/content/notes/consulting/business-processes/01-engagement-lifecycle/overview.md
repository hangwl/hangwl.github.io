# Engagement Lifecycle Overview

## Introduction

A Data and AI consulting engagement in consulting follows a structured lifecycle from initial client contact through project closure. This document provides a high-level overview of each phase.

## Lifecycle Phases

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  1. OPPORTUNITY   │  2. PROPOSAL   │  3. INITIATION  │  4. EXECUTION  │  5. CLOSURE  │
│     & SALES       │    & PRICING   │    & KICKOFF    │   & DELIVERY   │  & HANDOVER  │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

---

## Phase 1: Opportunity and Sales

**Duration:** Varies (days to months)

**Key Activities:**
- Client relationship development
- Opportunity identification and qualification
- Initial scope discussions
- Independence and conflict checks

**Key Outputs:**
- Opportunity registered in CRM (CRM)
- Initial client meeting notes
- Qualified opportunity assessment

**Key Stakeholders:**
- Business Development Lead
- Engagement Partner
- Client Relationship Partner

---

## Phase 2: Proposal and Pricing

**Duration:** 1-4 weeks typically

**Key Activities:**
- Requirements gathering and scoping
- Solution design and approach development
- Effort estimation and pricing
- Proposal document creation
- Internal reviews and approvals
- Client presentation and negotiation

**Key Outputs:**
- Statement of Work (SOW)
- Master Services Agreement (MSA)
- Engagement Letter
- Pricing model and fee structure

**Key Stakeholders:**
- Engagement Manager
- Solution Architect
- Pricing/Finance team
- Legal/Contracting

---

## Phase 3: Project Initiation and Kickoff

**Duration:** 1-2 weeks

**Key Activities:**
- Team mobilization and onboarding
- Client access and environment setup
- Project governance establishment
- Kickoff meeting
- Detailed project planning

**Key Outputs:**
- Project Charter
- Detailed Project Plan
- RACI Matrix
- Communication Plan
- Risk Register (initial)

**Key Stakeholders:**
- Project Manager / Engagement Manager
- Technical Lead
- Client Project Sponsor
- Client Project Manager

---

## Phase 4: Project Execution and Delivery

**Duration:** Varies based on engagement

**Key Activities:**
- Sprint/phase planning and execution
- Regular status reporting
- Deliverable development and review
- Quality assurance
- Change management
- Risk and issue management

**Key Outputs:**
- Sprint/Phase deliverables
- Status reports
- Technical documentation
- Client presentations
- Change requests (if applicable)

**Key Stakeholders:**
- Full project team
- Client stakeholders
- Quality reviewers

---

## Phase 5: Project Closure and Handover

**Duration:** 1-2 weeks

**Key Activities:**
- Final deliverable acceptance
- Knowledge transfer sessions
- Documentation handover
- Project retrospective
- Financial closeout
- Client satisfaction survey

**Key Outputs:**
- Final deliverables package
- Knowledge transfer documentation
- Lessons learned report
- Project closure report
- Client reference (if applicable)

**Key Stakeholders:**
- Engagement Partner
- Engagement Manager
- Client Sponsor
- Finance team

---

## Cross-Cutting Concerns

These activities span the entire engagement lifecycle:

| Concern | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Quality Management** | Continuous quality assurance and reviews |
| **Risk Management** | Ongoing identification and mitigation |
| **Independence & Ethics** | Compliance with the firm independence requirements |
| **Resource Management** | Team allocation and utilization |
| **Financial Management** | Budgeting, billing, and revenue recognition |
| **Change Management** | Scope and contract change processes |
